## Questions

### Question 1 — UX Scope (Foundational)

**What does UX design primarily concern?**

- A) Visual styling, typography, and colour palettes
- B) The complete experience — research, structure, emotion, and outcomes
- C) Writing interface microcopy and error messages only
- D) Frontend code implementation and deployment

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 2 — User-Centered Design (Foundational)

**Which statement best reflects user-centered design?**

- A) Stakeholder opinions should drive all design decisions
- B) Designers should trust their own intuition before talking to users
- C) Design decisions should be grounded in evidence from real users
- D) Visual brand consistency matters more than user needs

**Correct answer: C**

---

### Question 3 — Research Types (Foundational)

**Usability testing a clickable prototype to see if users can complete a task is an example of:**

- A) Generative research
- B) Evaluative research
- C) Competitive analysis
- D) Heuristic evaluation

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 4 — Observation vs Insight (Foundational)

**Which of the following is an *insight*, not just an observation?**

- A) "42% of users dropped off at step 3 of the form"
- B) "Users abandon because the shipping cost only appears at the final step"
- C) "We conducted eight user interviews this week"
- D) "The primary action button is positioned below the fold"

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 5 — Nielsen Heuristics (Foundational)

**A loading screen with a spinner but no estimated time or progress indicator most directly violates which Nielsen heuristic?**

- A) Consistency and standards
- B) Visibility of system status
- C) Aesthetic and minimalist design
- D) Recognition rather than recall

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 6 — Double Diamond (Foundational)

**In the Double Diamond process, the first diamond (Discover → Define) is primarily about:**

- A) Shipping the highest-fidelity prototype as fast as possible
- B) Understanding the problem and framing the right question to solve
- C) Writing production-ready code for developers
- D) Finalising visual design and brand assets

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 7 — Interview Technique (Foundational)

**Which is a *leading* interview question?**

- A) "Tell me about the last time you tried to complete this task."
- B) "What was the hardest part of that process for you?"
- C) "Was the checkout process confusing for you?"
- D) "What did you do when you got stuck?"

**Correct answer: C**

---

### Question 8 — Jobs to Be Done (Intermediate)

**Which is the best-formed JTBD (Jobs to Be Done) statement?**

- A) "Users aged 25–40 want a faster mobile application"
- B) "When I need to submit a government permit, I want to know exactly which documents are required, so I can complete it in one visit"
- C) "We should add a progress bar to the multi-step form"
- D) "Citizens prefer using mobile devices over desktop computers"

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 9 — FigJam vs Figma (Intermediate)

**After conducting user interviews, the best tool for affinity-mapping sticky notes into insight clusters is:**

- A) Figma — because it supports high-fidelity mockups
- B) FigJam — because it is built for collaborative thinking and synthesis
- C) Claude — because it replaces the need for a whiteboard
- D) Perplexity — because it is designed for wireframing

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 10 — AI Tool Roles (Intermediate)

**In UX research, Perplexity is best used for:**

- A) Generating final persona quotes without checking sources
- B) Conducting cited competitive scans and trend research
- C) Replacing the need for user interviews entirely
- D) Automatically generating production-ready CSS

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 11 — AI Synthesis (Intermediate)

**Claude is best suited for which research task?**

- A) Real-time API integration with government databases
- B) Clustering and synthesising interview notes into themes — with mandatory human review
- C) Automated deployment of designs to production servers
- D) Certifying WCAG legal compliance for a product

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 12 — AI Reliability (Intermediate)

**Before adding AI-generated research themes to a stakeholder presentation, you should:**

- A) Publish immediately — AI saves time and is generally accurate
- B) Verify each theme against source data and flag anything inferred vs evidenced
- C) Remove participant names but keep quotes even if you cannot trace them to a source
- D) Trust the output because the model was trained on UX best practices

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 13 — Data Privacy (Intermediate)

**Which data should *never* be pasted into a third-party AI tool like Claude or ChatGPT?**

- A) Public marketing copy from your organisation's website
- B) Anonymised, aggregated task-completion statistics
- C) Raw citizen national ID numbers and full application records
- D) Generic Nielsen heuristic reference material

**Correct answer: C**

---

### Question 14 — Accessibility (Advanced)

**In WCAG, the acronym POUR stands for:**

- A) Plan, Organise, Upload, Release
- B) Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust
- C) Privacy, Ownership, Usability, Reliability
- D) Process, Output, User, Research

**Correct answer: B**

---

### Question 15 — Responsible AI in Government (Advanced)

**Under the EU AI Act, an AI system that automatically scores citizens for government benefits eligibility is typically classified as:**

- A) Minimal risk — standard UX process, no extra requirements
- B) High risk — requiring human oversight, documentation, and impact assessment
- C) Unacceptable risk — banned in all circumstances without exception
- D) No risk — UX designers do not need to consider regulatory classification

**Correct answer: B**
